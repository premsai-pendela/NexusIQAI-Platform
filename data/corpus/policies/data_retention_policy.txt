DATA RETENTION AND ACCESS POLICY

Effective December 1, 2024. Owner: Data Governance Council. Applies to all
NexusIQ operational and analytical data stores.

RETENTION SCHEDULE

Transactional records (sales_transactions, returns): retained 7 years to
satisfy financial audit requirements, then archived to cold storage for a
further 3 years before deletion.

Customer account records: retained while the account is active plus 3
years after closure, unless a deletion request is received under an
applicable privacy regulation, in which case deletion completes within 30
days and is logged in the privacy register.

Support tickets: retained 3 years from closure. Tickets attached to an
open corrective action (such as CAPA-2024-091) are retained until the
corrective action closes, regardless of age.

Analytical extracts and BI caches: retained 13 months, covering
year-over-year comparison, then purged on the monthly maintenance window.

ACCESS TIERS

Tier A (Finance, Data Governance): full row-level access to transactional
and returns data, including refund amounts.

Tier B (Operations, Support leadership): transactional access with
customer identifiers pseudonymized outside an active ticket context.

Tier C (General analytics users): aggregate access only through the
governed metrics layer; no row-level export.

Access reviews run quarterly. Dormant accounts are disabled after 90 days
without login. All access changes are logged in the audit trail with actor,
approver, and timestamp.

EXCEPTIONS

Exceptions require Data Governance Council approval, an expiry date not
exceeding 12 months, and quarterly re-attestation. Emergency access during
an incident may be granted for 72 hours by the on-call data platform lead
and is reviewed at the next council meeting.
