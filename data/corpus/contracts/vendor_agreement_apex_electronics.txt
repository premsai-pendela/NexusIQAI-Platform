MASTER VENDOR AGREEMENT (EXCERPT FOR OPERATIONS USE)

Parties: NexusIQ Retail Operations ("Buyer") and Apex Electronics Supply
Co. ("Vendor"). Effective November 15, 2024. Term: 24 months with one
12-month renewal option. This operations excerpt omits pricing schedules
held by Finance.

SECTION 4 — SUPPLY AND FULFILLMENT

4.1 Vendor supplies the Electronics category SKUs listed in Exhibit A
(laptops, tablets, phones, monitors, audio) to the four NexusIQ regional
warehouses.

4.2 Standard lead time is 21 calendar days from purchase order to
warehouse receipt. Orders flagged expedite ship within 10 calendar days at
a 6% logistics surcharge.

4.3 Fill rate commitment: 96% of ordered units per quarter. A fill rate
below 92% in any quarter is a material breach subject to Section 11
remedies.

SECTION 7 — QUALITY AND DEFECTS

7.1 Vendor warrants units against manufacturing defects for 12 months from
warehouse receipt.

7.2 Dead-on-arrival (DOA) claims and defect patterns identified by Buyer's
Risk Operations are escalated through the vendor quality channel. Vendor
must acknowledge within 3 business days and file a corrective action plan
within 15 business days for any defect pattern exceeding 50 affected units.
The September 2024 accelerometer calibration corrective action
(CAPA-2024-091) was processed under this clause.

7.3 Defective returns accepted by Buyer under its customer policy are
credited by Vendor at landed cost plus a $12 per-unit handling allowance,
settled monthly.

SECTION 9 — DATA AND REPORTING

9.1 Vendor provides weekly inventory availability feeds and monthly quality
summaries in the agreed CSV format. Feeds later than 5 business days in a
month trigger a service credit of 0.5% of that month's invoiced amount.

SECTION 11 — REMEDIES (SUMMARY)

Material breach remedies include order reallocation to alternate vendors,
service credits, and termination with 60 days notice after an uncured
breach. Disputes route to executive escalation before arbitration.
